--- layout: china_post name: lao-long-tou title: Lao Long Tou time: 2006-09-25 22:49:00.000000000 -07:00 --- A couple of Saturdays ago (on September 16th, to be exact) I visited Lao Long Tou (which means "Old Dragon's Head"). Lao Long Tou is the starting point of the great wall--the point where the great wall hits the sea. It's called "Old Dragon's Head" because the great wall is sometimes called a dragon since it snakes across the landscape like a dragon, and this is the starting point--hence, it's the Dragon's Head. It was built during the Ming Dynasty in the 16th century.

I went to Lao Long Tou with Chad & Krystal--a married couple from Oklahoma who are also teaching at QFLPC with ELT Edge. We brought with us a couple of my students (Rose & Anna) to help translate for us and get us there and back. Anyhow, on to some pictures with captions:
